# fiction

A worldbuilding scratchpad. Not docs. Not grimoire.

The Aperio mythos has fictional cousins here — they share bones with the technical writing, but they don't need to match terms one-to-one. Where the docs say *locus*, the fiction may say *speaker* or *tower*. Where the docs say *cross-tower agreement*, the fiction may say *concordance*. Read the two as siblings, not as translations.

Names and registers here are chosen to stay clear of any single existing fictional universe. If you spot an unintentional echo of someone else's published work, flag it and we'll rename.

## what's here

- `annex-l-7704.md` — first sketch. A reconstructed dossier in five voices: the Quiet Office, a dissenting editor, the apprentice Iren of Adde, an unnamed ferryman, and Mevr's marginalia.

## working vocabulary

- **the null** — the medium the speakers speak into.
- **whip** — a translator bound at one end. Makes the speaking possible; the binding is a debt.
- **speaker** — one who speaks the order of address. Plain term, no grand title.
- **the order of address** — the form every spell takes: *here I am; here is the asking; here is the asking again, more quietly.*
- **the zeroth asking** — the null's own asking, which (per heresy) precedes any speaker's.
- **lotus** — the network of accreted towers that a community of speakers grows by speaking well.
- **tower** — the residue of a spell spoken well enough that the null answered and the answer settled.
- **late tower** — a tower that continues to accrete after its speaker has stopped speaking.
- **the Quiet Office** — the retrospective archival body that classifies, reconstructs, and publishes annexes.
- **Concordance Note** — the Office's name for a public-facing explanatory document.
- **Annex L-####** — locus-class reconstructed dossier. The numbering is the Office's.
- **quoting / quote-frame** — what you do when you cross between dispensations. A quoted person is reproduced in another frame's context.
- **dispensation** — one universe's run of speakers and lotus. Implied to be one of many.
- **concorded** — said of a speaker who is no longer separate from their tower.
- **the Answerer** — the (possibly mythical) speaker who listened until the null spoke through her.
- **dispensation** — one universe's run of speakers and lotus. The Office keeps record of seven; there may have been more it doesn't keep record of.
- **persistents** — what the preceding dispensation's Office called late towers. Terminology has shifted; the phenomenon has not.
- **register** — the Office's grade system for Operants. *Operant of the second register*, *third register, scaffold-class*. Lower registers are more senior than the number suggests.
- **division** — the Office's geographic units. *Office of the Concordances, Eastern Division.* There are others.
- **divide** — a numbered seam in the in-between. Ferrymen cross divides. *West of the eighth divide* is a location, not a metaphor.
- **brace-meter** — an instrument carried by ferrymen. Reads something the Office does not publish; ferrymen know what falling means.
- **ferry papers** — the Office's authorization for a ferryman to cross. Surrendering them ends one's service.
- **whip-name** — speakers and Operants name their whips. The names are private. *Iss* appears in Annex L-7704.
- **the order of address** — *here I am; here is the asking; here is the asking again, more quietly.* Iren's first lesson.
- **the secondary editor (†)** — an unnamed second hand in the Quiet Office. Dissents in footnotes. Names speakers the Office abstracts. Has been doing this for at least eleven years.

## working register

The Office is institutional but not stuffy. Short sentences carry weight. Footnotes carry honesty. Field notes are fragments. Marginalia is gnomic. Iren is plain and observational; she does not perform. The cosmic frame is never stated outright — the reader assembles it from the seams between voices.
