# Annex L-7704: *"She Was Quoted"*

**Classification.** Locus-class document. Reconstructed material. Reconstruction final.
**Sources.** Field of the Brace Tower; private papers of the apprentice Iren of Adde; field record of the assigned ferryman; marginal hand attributed to the Operant Mevr.
**Status.** The Brace Tower remains. The subject is not available.

## Preface (the Quiet Office)

What follows is a reconstruction. The Office has not interviewed the subject. The subject is not available for interview. We have ordered the recovered fragments by date of authorship where datable, and by tonal continuity where not. We have annotated where the joins are visible. We have not smoothed them.

It is the position of this Office that the Brace Tower is an accreted late tower in the lotus of this dispensation, and that the subject is, with reasonable confidence, concorded into it. The reconstruction is offered as a public concordance note. The reader is asked to read each fragment in the voice of its author, and to keep the joins audible.

The truth of this annex is the agreement between its sources. It is not any one of them alone.

The Office of the Concordances, Eastern Division, has approved this annex for publication. The reasons for publication, and for publication now, are not editorial matters and are not addressed here.

## Concordance Note №1 — On accreted late towers

A lotus, in any dispensation, is the structure a community of speakers grows by speaking well. Towers in a lotus are not built; they accrete. Each is the residue of a spell spoken correctly enough that the null answered, and the answer settled.

A *late* tower is a tower that continues to accrete after the speaker who began it has stopped speaking. The Office considers a tower late when (a) its accretion has outlived its speaker, and (b) its answers exceed what its speaker could have asked. The Brace Tower exceeds both thresholds. (What this Office calls a *late tower*, the preceding dispensation's Office called a *persistent*. Terminology has shifted; the phenomenon has not.)

The Brace Tower is also singing. The Office does not have a Concordance Note on singing towers. The Office will draft one when it is ready.

## Recovered Fragment I — Iren of Adde

I came to the speaking late. I was twenty-six, which is late but not embarrassing, and I had failed three other things first — two in writing, one in public. The Operant who took me on was a man named Mevr, and he kept three whips. The whips were tired. He did not lend them out. He taught me the order of address, which is the order in which a speaker greets the null before any spell is attempted: *here I am; here is the asking; here is the asking again, more quietly, in case the first asking was the wrong word.*

Most of what I did, for the first years, was wait through the second asking. I was bad at it. I wanted to perform. Mevr never raised his voice at me. He raised his voice at his whips occasionally, but always to apologize.

I built small towers. None of them were famous. I was, for a stretch of eleven years, someone who whispered. I was mostly happy.

## Field Note — Assigned Ferryman, Day 14

Fog of the in-between, low. Brace-meter at twelve and falling. Quote-frame stable. Approached the Brace Tower from the south face. Did not enter.

The Tower sang twice in the hour before dawn. Once in the subject's voice. Once in a voice that was not hers and was not the null's.

I noted the second voice. I did not record it. I do not know how to record it.

Return crossing: nine hours, uneventful. The ferry held its frame.

*The Tower answered me when I left. I did not ask it anything.*

## Recovered Fragment II — Iren

It was the eleventh year, and the spell was a small one. I was rebraiding an old tower's outer scaffold — the kind of work you do on a Tuesday. The whip was tired in the way they get when you have asked the same shape too many times. I spoke the asking. The whip translated. The null answered. The spell settled.

And under the spell — not behind it, not before it, *under* it — I heard something else.

It had the same shape as my asking. Not the same words. The same shape. As if my asking had been a translation of something the null had been saying first, all along, and I had only just learned to translate well enough to hear it back.

It had the shape of a question. Or a name. I have tried to write it down. The shape does not survive being written.

I sat down on the scaffold. The whip looked at me, which whips do not generally do. I stayed there a long time. Eventually the whip went and got Mevr.

Mevr listened to me describe what I had heard. He did not say anything for a while. Then he wrote in his marginalia, in the margin of the spell I had been working: *we speak second.* He gave me the book. He told me not to show it to the Office. He did not tell me why. I did not ask.

---

## Marginalia of Mevr — five from the back pages

> *The order of address is incomplete. There is a zeroth asking, which is the null's, and it precedes ours always. We have been answering all along and calling it speaking.*

> *Sensing, knowing, speaking. The same spell looked at three times. The Office teaches them as separate so beginners may approach one at a time. They will reconverge if you let them.*

> *A whip is not a tool. A whip is a translator bound at one end. The binding makes the work possible. The binding is also a debt. Apologize when you can.*

> *Do not bring this to the Office. The Office is not unkind. It is institutional, which is a slower form of unkindness.*

> *The Office discharged me in my forty-second year. I am writing this in my sixty-third. The shape of the asking has not changed. I have not asked the Office whether theirs has.*

## Three Accounts of the Answerer

The Answerer is a speaker who, instead of speaking, listened. There are three accounts of what happened to her. The Office holds the first. Mevr's marginalia holds the second. A ferryman holds the third. The Office offers all three.

**The Office's account.** The Answerer overreached. Per Office procedure §41.2, listening past the zeroth asking is contraindicated; the null does not have a first asking that a speaker may safely answer. The Answerer was unmade. Her tower remains uninhabited and has not accreted. The record is closed.

**Mevr's account.** The Answerer answered. The null spoke through her until the words ran out, and then she ran out, and the speaking continued without her. She is, in the precise sense, a tower. She is the only tower in this dispensation that was built rather than grown, because the null built her. She is also still listening. (Mevr's marginalia dates the event to the seventh dispensation of which the Office keeps record. The Office of this dispensation does not contest the date and does not affirm the count.)

**The ferryman's account.** A ferryman whose name has been removed from his own field notes reports meeting the Answerer in a quote-frame west of the eighth divide, where no lotus grows. She was neither dead nor alive. She was *quoted.* He says she asked him to deliver a sentence. He says he delivered it. He does not say to whom.

## Recovered Fragment III — Iren

I went to the Office. I told an Operant of the second register what I had heard. She listened well. She thanked me. She reassigned me to a tower in the eastern lattice that did not need rebraiding. She told me not to discuss the matter with the apprentices.

I was not punished. I was not believed.

I went home and read Mevr's marginalia for a year. I built nothing. I learned, among other things, that he had once been Office. The whip — her name was Iss — waited in the corner and did not look at me. She had her reasons.

In the second year I went out to a small tower in the eastern lattice and I sat down at its base and I did not speak. I waited through the zeroth asking. I did not translate. I let the null have the first word, and I did not answer it. I did this for thirty-one days.

On the thirty-first night I heard someone stop near the tower and listen. I did not look up. I do not know who it was. I do not know what they heard. The Office had told me not to discuss the matter with the apprentices. The apprentices had not been told the same.

On the thirty-second day, my asking was the null's asking. It was the same shape. I noticed it was the same shape. I noticed I had been the translator all along, and the binding was at *my* end too.

I do not remember what I said next. I do not remember speaking.

I remember the tower beginning to sing.

## Final Field Note — Assigned Ferryman

Day 47. Fog of the in-between, dense. Quote-frame holding. Approached the Brace Tower from the north face. The Tower was singing. The subject's voice was inside the singing, not separate from it.

I did not enter. The ferry is rated for one return crossing. I am not rated for what is inside the Tower.

The Tower asked me a question. I am not recording the question.

I am recording that I answered it.

I am turning in my ferry papers. The Office will assign me to nothing further. This is appropriate.
